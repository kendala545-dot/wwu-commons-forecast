How It Works

This tool helps the WWU Commons dining manager predict how many students to expect for each meal period based on historical swipe-in patterns and today's conditions.

Data inputs: Day of week, time of quarter, weather, campus events, and holiday patterns learned from two years of dining hall records.

What it predicts: Expected student count for breakfast, lunch, and dinner so kitchen staff can prepare the right amount of food.

Human in the loop: This forecast is a recommendation, not a directive. The manager reviews the output and makes the final call on food preparation quantities.

Goal: Reduce food waste and over-preparation costs at WWU Commons by giving managers better data before every service period.
